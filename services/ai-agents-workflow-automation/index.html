Frequently asked questions

What are AI agents?

AI agents are systems that can plan steps, use tools, retrieve context and take actions within defined permissions—often with human approval for high-stakes decisions.

How are AI agents different from workflow automation?

Classic automation follows fixed rules. Agents handle variable inputs and multi-step reasoning—but require stronger governance, observability and human-in-the-loop controls.

What is human-in-the-loop automation?

It keeps humans accountable for approvals, exceptions and audit—while AI handles drafting, routing, extraction and recommendations within clear boundaries.

Can AI agents be used safely in healthcare or insurance workflows?

Yes, when designed around workflow truth, permissions, escalation paths and audit trails—not unbounded autonomy in regulated operations.

How do you decide what an agent should automate?

By mapping tasks for value, blast radius, data quality and exception rate—then sequencing pilots where tool access and HITL are well defined.

Do you help design agentic workflows before development starts?

Yes. The engagement produces blueprints, responsibility matrices and MVP scope before engineering sprint zero.
