HealthPay-BajajPay QR Transition & Payment Migration

Case study: Migrating provider QR payment infrastructure to BajajPay, defining integration and onboarding designs for secure merchant onboarding.

Context

In 2023, during the Partner Center rollout, HealthPay undertook a major migration project to transition its payment QR infrastructure to BajajPay QR. The scope spanned provider onboarding, QR generation, transaction feeds, PG settlements, refunds, and support operations across doctors, labs, and hospital partners.

Business problem

HealthPay needed to merge its provider-side payment collection infrastructure with BajajPay (BFL). The change affected how QR codes were mapped, how PG settlement credits were tracked, and how refund webhooks operated.

First Principles Analysis: The challenge was not just coding APIs, but managing payment infrastructure migration without operational disruption. We reframed this from a simple QR replacement to a controlled cross-company payment infrastructure transition. It required mapping precise workflows for provider onboarding, QR mapping/logistics, settlement reconciliation, and refund triggers to ensure operational continuity.

User or operational pain

A direct, unmanaged cutover could result in lost transactions, merchant onboarding errors, and settlement delays. Operations and logistics teams needed clear processes to manage QR distributions, and relationship managers required tools to handle provider concerns during the transition.

Product intervention

We designed the QR transition integration architecture:

  • QR Logistics and Onboarding: Defined QR generation, center mapping, and onboarding flows.
  • Reconciliation Integration: Synced BajajPay transaction and settlement feeds with PayRx.
  • Refund Workflows: Programmed webhook triggers to handle partial and full refunds.
  • Support & Continuity SOPs: Created backup workflows for finance and operations during the migration.

AI capability used

The system integrated API-led transaction hooks, settlement matching webhooks, and automated refund validations.

Samadhan's role

Samadhan worked on the merger in parallel to Partner Center. He was responsible for product requirements definition, solutioning, integration coordinates, QR migration planning, logistics SOP, and UAT support until transitioning to another project in the final phases.

Business outcome

The integration between BajajPay and HealthPay was successfully completed, UAT was passed, and the codebase was deployed to production, establishing the foundation for provider QR migration.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Ownership boundaries: In multi-company integrations, define clear ownership boundaries (e.g., who owns provider communication vs payout rails) to avoid execution blocks.
  2. Design for operational fallbacks: Build manual verification loops into UAT plans to handle edge cases where webhook events are dropped.
