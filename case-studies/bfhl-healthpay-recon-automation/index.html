HealthPay Recon & Settlement Automation

Case study: Automating multi-ledger pay-in and pay-out reconciliation for doctor and lab payouts, enabling T+1 payments via RazorpayX.

Context

Executed in 2023 immediately after the Service Guarantee project, the HealthPay Recon & Settlement Automation initiative focused on automating pay-in and pay-out reconciliation across customer QR scans, corporate benefit utilization (vouchers, healthcoins, claims), payment gateway settlements, and provider payouts (doctors, labs).

Business problem

HealthPay transactions were not simple single-ledger payments. A single transaction involved net payments, Voucherify redemptions, Healthcoin burns, claims discounts, payment gateway fees, service taxes, and provider payouts. Reconciling these across PayU, RazorPay, PayRx, HRx, DRx, Voucherify, and bank statements required manual verification, resulting in slow operations and high settlement risk.

First Principles Analysis: Reconciliation cannot rely on a single gateway ledger. The matching engine needed to understand value composition: Gross Transaction Value = Net Customer Paid + Voucher + Healthcoin + Claim Benefit. The payout calculation had to be dynamically triggered based on pay-in matching: Final Payout = Net Received + Benefits Applied + PG Charges. Payouts had to be triggered programmatically via RazorpayX APIs only after successful matching.

User or operational pain

Finance teams spent hours matching daily records, delaying doctor and lab payouts. Delayed settlements led to severe provider escalations and relationship strain. Manual correction of raw ledger entries lacked audit trails and authorization, creating high financial controllership risks.

Product intervention

We built a multi-layered financial operations platform:

  • API Ingestion Layer: Automated ingestion of feeds from PayU, RazorPay, Voucherify, Claims, and bank statements, retaining manual upload as a fallback.
  • Reconciliation Engine (PayRx): Ran multi-ledger matching rules to reconcile transaction count and amount details.
  • Governed Manual Review: Surfaced exceptions (amount mismatches, failed payouts, beneficiary bank issues) in specialized queues.
  • Governance Layer: Implemented role-based edit permissions, maker-checker approvals for edited ledger data, and comprehensive audit logs.

AI capability used

Reconciliation algorithms matched multi-ledger balances, flagged count and value anomalies, and triggered RazorpayX payouts programmatically.

Samadhan's role

Samadhan was Lead PM for the complete project: owned the PRD, user stories, matching engine rules, governance controls, and participated in daily operations review until stabilization.

Business outcome

The matching engine successfully went live, delivering substantial operational impact:

  • Manual reconciliation effort was eliminated as the process became fully automated.
  • Payouts to doctors and labs were completed on a T+1 cycle.
  • Payout delays and provider escalations reduced, and financial controllership was strengthened through audit trails.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Build for resilience: API failures occur. Retaining manual upload fallbacks and designing clear contingency plans for payment gateway downtime prevents operational paralysis.
  2. Governance is not a feature; it is core: Multi-ledger matching engines need role-based edits and maker-checker validation to satisfy audit, risk, and compliance standards.
