BFHL Wellness Cohort Engagement & Cost Optimization

Case study: Building an integrated wellness engagement and care-delivery portfolio, saving INR 95 lakhs by replacing external dietician vendors with a custom platform.

Context

From January 2024 to September 2024, Samadhan led the BFHL Wellness Cohort. This assignment was a portfolio-level product leadership role covering Activity/Step Tracker, Diet & Nutrition, Weight Management, and Fitness, aimed at corporate Group Medical Cover (GMC) retention and wellness revenue generation.

Business problem

Generic health tips and passive step trackers failed to drive active engagement or justify corporate health plan purchases. Furthermore, offering diet consultations through third-party external vendors was cost-inefficient, lacked quality control, and did not support structured follow-up care.

First Principles Analysis: Wellness cannot be solved through content alone. Users need motivation, expert guidance, follow-up accountability, and progress tracking. We reframed the approach: from isolated wellness features to an integrated wellness operating model. This meant replacing third-party dietician vendors with a custom in-house dietician platform and care workflow, while gamifying physical activity through themed challenge journeys.

User or operational pain

Corporate clients demanded measurable employee participation data to justify renewals. Users signed up but rapidly dropped off. Operational teams struggled to coordinate diet schedules and manage records without structured workflows or SLAs.

Product intervention

We built a dual-pillar wellness platform:

  • Custom Dietician Platform: Built an in-house platform covering consultation booking, meal planning, EMR records, chat support, and follow-up scheduling. Defined strict service SLAs (diet plan creation within 48 hours, chat replies within 12 hours).
  • Gamified Activity Engine: Launched gamified step challenges based on virtual treks (Everest, Kilimanjaro) featuring rewards, badges, leaderboards, team formats, and peer-to-peer nudges.

AI capability used

The gamified engine utilized step-tracking aggregation algorithms, behavioral reminders, and automated recovery notifications for missed diet consultations.

Samadhan's role

As Lead PM, Samadhan owned the entire Wellness cohort. He managed a reporting squad of 5 PMs (Maaz Modak, Saumya Shukla, Pragya Kumpawat, Aditi Rana, Mahak Sonekar), defined dietician consultation SOPs, designed the custom platform features, and aligned the product to GMC business targets.

Business outcome

The Wellness platform delivered high business and financial impact:

  • The custom dietician platform went live, fully replacing external vendors and **saving INR 95 lakhs** in costs.
  • Gamified activity challenges (Everest, Kilimanjaro) launched, driving active user counts.
  • Built a sustainable care-delivery model with structured follow-ups and EMR record keeping.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Insource strategic capabilities: Moving from third-party dietician vendors to a custom in-house platform not only saved INR 95 lakhs but also enabled full control over service quality and user data.
  2. Gamification requires social triggers: Simple step logs are boring. Adding virtual routes, team challenges, and peer nudges creates viral loops that sustain physical activity.
