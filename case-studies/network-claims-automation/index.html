AI-Led Network Claims Automation Workflow

Case study: mapping and automating network claims, OPD, FinOps, and audit workflows with agentic patterns and governed tool use.

Context

The Network Claims modernization initiative focused on automating post-discharge claims, billing reconciliation, and insurer concurrence. This workflow operates in a high-volume TPA environment where claims must be reconciled against provider agreements, insurer packages, and historical pre-authorizations before final settlement.

Business problem

End-to-end claim visibility was weak. Processors manually planned their days, opened allocated claims from dumps, and cross-referenced bills against tariff booklets. Tariff deviations, package exclusions, and implant invoice discrepancies were processed manually, resulting in long payment cycles and financial leakage.

First Principles Analysis: The challenge was not just document extraction, but the lack of dynamic tool execution. Processors spent excessive time fetching files and manually compiling insurer concurrence packets. We reframed this as an agentic tool integration problem: decomposing claim review tasks into actions that could be performed by automated scripts with structured inputs and rollback capabilities.

User or operational pain

Claim processors suffered from high manual effort and lack of daily workload organization. Operations experienced high error rates in billing capture and room category matching, causing high reclaim volume, audit findings, and provider relationship strain.

Product intervention

We designed a structured Claims Processing operating platform:

  • Auto-Allocation and Planning: Replaced spreadsheet allocation with intelligent queues, tagging cases by complexity and urgency.
  • Checklist-Driven Review: Built interactive checklists for document, medical, and rule checking.
  • Claim Letter & Concurrence Automation: Automatically compiles the final approval letter and maps insurer concurrence details, creating ready-to-send email packages.

AI capability used

Deploying agentic patterns, the system uses tool-calling agents to perform validation runs, RAG over policy documents, and structured extraction of implant invoice values.

Samadhan's role

Samadhan led the product discovery and solutioning. He mapped the complete Network Claims task journey (from planning the day to final insurer concurrence revert tracking), defined validation checklist rules, and designed dashboard interfaces for queue tracking and productivity management.

Business outcome

The project delivered a structured claims validation workflow. It significantly reduced billing errors, automated the generation of approval letters and concurrence packages, and provided operations managers with real-time capacity and productivity insights.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Map the workflow before buying models: Automation success is bounded by tool quality and data integration, not prompt structure.
  2. Control the blast radius: Enable tool-calling agents to perform actions in sandbox environments with explicit human approvals before final writes.
