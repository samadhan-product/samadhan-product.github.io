Scaling Institutional EdTech SaaS Portfolios — SREM & Forms Dot Star

How SREM and Forms Dot Star digitized admissions, student engagement, application processing, payments, and analytics for 800+ educational institutions in India.

Context

At Creanovation Labs / Creanovation Technologies, Samadhan Mishra served as the Product Leader and end-to-end Product Owner for a dual-product EdTech SaaS portfolio. The products—SREM (Student Relationship & Engagement Management) and Forms Dot Star—were built to modernize enrollment, admission workflows, payments, and student life-cycles for colleges, universities, and coaching institutes across India.

Business problem

The Indian higher education sector faced highly fragmented, paper-heavy, and manual admission processes. Admissions offices struggled with uncoordinated lead follow-ups, offline application processing, and manual verification of student marksheets. Institutions had diverse, non-standardized admission rules, making the deployment of rigid software structures impossible.

First Principles Analysis: The challenge was not just building online forms, but building a highly configurable SaaS workflow operating system. The platform had to allow each university to customize their workflows, dynamic payment options, and communication rules while keeping the core application framework unified and scalable.

User or operational pain

Institutions suffered from low visibility into conversion funnels and poor lead follow-up discipline, leading to high inquiry drop-offs. Manual document checks slowed application cycles from weeks to months, and offline fee collection was a major administrative burden. Counseling teams experienced cognitive overload from managing paper records and spreadsheets.

Product intervention

We designed and launched modular, configurable SaaS tracks across both platforms:

  • Intelligent Lead Scoring (SREM): Prioritized high-intent student leads for counselors.
  • Automated Admission Workflow (Forms Dot Star): Guided student applications from ingestion to fee payment.
  • White-Labeled Portals: Enabled institutions to customize forms, branding, and permissions.
  • Multi-Channel Communication: Integrated SMS, email, and WhatsApp notifications.

AI capability used

The technical innovation focused on automated workflows, incorporating basic lead-scoring algorithms, automated communication templates, and OCR-based document scanning to extract marksheet details.

Samadhan's role

Samadhan led the product strategy, roadmap planning, and cross-functional execution. He built and mentored an 8+ member cross-functional squad across engineering, design, and customer success, managing the product lifecycle from initial discovery to deployment and scaling.

Business outcome

The EdTech SaaS portfolio achieved significant scale and commercial success:

  • SREM scaled from 150 to 500+ institutional clients (233% growth in 2.5 years), processing 2M+ annual student interactions and generating INR 15Cr+ in annual platform revenue.
  • Forms Dot Star served 300+ colleges and universities across 15+ states, processing 500K+ applications annually and generating INR 8Cr+ ARR.
  • Application processing time reduced from 7 days to 2 days, and document verification time decreased by 70%.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Configurability is a B2B SaaS superpower: In highly fragmented industries like Indian education, standard solutions fail. Build configurable workflow blocks to support diverse user policies.
  2. Measure the operational velocity: Shift metrics from application volume to cycle-time reduction (reducing document verification time by 70%). This is what drives software renewals.
