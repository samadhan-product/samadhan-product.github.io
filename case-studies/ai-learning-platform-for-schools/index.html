Chapter 01

Context

This case study covers a B2B EdTech initiative designed to bring structured, LMS-aligned AI education to K-12 institutions. The project focused on building an educational ecosystem that combines teacher enablement, project-based student learning, and administrative oversight.

Chapter 02

Business problem

Schools attempted to introduce AI literacy through ad-hoc workshops and unstructured consumer tools (like ChatGPT), which lacked age-appropriate boundaries, pedagogical structure, and progress tracking.

First Principles Analysis: Students do not need another chatbot; they need a guided, project-based curriculum where they learn AI mechanics (prompting, RAG, simple agent behavior) in a sandbox environment. The product had to balance open-ended exploration with institutional safety, teacher playbooks, and school grading rubrics.

Chapter 03

User or operational pain

School administrators could not justify program spending due to a lack of measurable outcomes. Teachers lacked AI expertise and struggled to run modules, while parents and school boards expressed safety concerns regarding unstructured web access.

Chapter 04

Product intervention

We designed a B2B AI Learning Platform concept:

  • LMS-Integrated Curriculum: Built progressive learning paths with guided projects, worksheets, and online assessments.
  • Teacher Enablement Portal: Surfaced ready-to-run lesson playbooks, automated grading rubrics, and engagement analytics.
  • Student Sandbox: Created sandbox workspaces with built-in prompt templates and restricted API boundaries.
Chapter 05

AI capability used

The platform integrated safe-boundary API endpoints, automated code templates, and analytical tracking of engagement.

Chapter 06

Samadhan's role

Samadhan led the product definition, stakeholder discovery, package design, and go-to-market narrative. He mapped school administration procurement cycles, defined safety requirements, and designed curriculum frameworks.

Chapter 07

Business outcome

Delivered a comprehensive product architecture and GTM blueprint. Secured initial school pilot commitments, defined teacher training playbooks, and established a clear safety model for school environments.

Chapter 08

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Safety is a core B2B value: B2B EdTech products succeed when they prioritize compliance, data privacy, and guided boundaries over raw feature performance.
  2. Enable the educator: The teacher is the primary user. Build tools that make it simple for non-technical teachers to run advanced AI lessons confidently.
