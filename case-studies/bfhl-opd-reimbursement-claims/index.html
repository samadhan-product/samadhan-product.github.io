OPD Reimbursement Claims Processing Automation

Case study: Revamping OPD reimbursement claims processing on Salesforce to increase processor capacity by 30% through automated rule engine and Customer 360 views.

Context

In 2022, Bajaj Finserv Health Limited (BFHL) undertook a major transformation of its OPD Reimbursement Claims Processing platform. Running on Salesforce Service Cloud, this system handles the intake of claims submitted via emails, consumer apps, and provider portals, routing them to claims processing desks.

Business problem

The existing claim processing was slow and heavily dependent on manual screen navigation. Processors spent significant time searching for customer profiles, pulling documents, verifying eligibility limits, and checking for duplicate submissions. Every customer email created a new Service Request (SR), leading to duplicates across multiple channels that went undetected.

First Principles Analysis: The block was not the database speed, but cognitive switching. Processors had to open up to 6 different Salesforce tabs to make a single claim decision. The solution required designing a single Customer 360 view that unified client history, policy limits, active benefits, and document viewers into one screen, coupled with an automated rules engine.

User or operational pain

Claim processor capacity was capped at 75 claims per day per agent, causing backlogs. Manual validation led to inconsistent policy rule applications and high audit error rates. Duplicate payments caused financial leakage, and the customer experience suffered from long settlement cycles.

Product intervention

We designed and shipped a unified claims operations interface on Salesforce:

  • Customer 360 Console: Consolidated policy data, transaction histories, active benefits, and case documents into a single page.
  • Round-Robin Auto-Allocation: Automated claim routing, prioritization, and SLA-based queues.
  • Configurable Rules Engine: Automated rules checking eligibility, document completeness, policy benefits, and duplicate checks.

AI capability used

The rules engine automated the verification of standard claim criteria, and basic NLP matching algorithms were implemented to scan active claims for potential duplicate indicators.

Samadhan's role

Samadhan led the product discovery and requirement specifications (PRS). He visited the call center, sat directly with claims processing agents to map workflow friction points, analyzed intake forms, and designed the Salesforce capability tracks.

Business outcome

The transformation delivered outstanding operational metrics:

  • Processor productivity increased from 75 to 130 claims/day (a 30% efficiency improvement achieved).
  • Straight-Through Processing (STP) rate reached 50%.
  • Decision accuracy reached 99%, with agent NPS improving to 40.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Sit with your users: Floor-level discovery reveals friction points (like manual screen switching and local MS Paint usage) that standard telemetry missed.
  2. Automate the checks, empower the human: Rule engines should automatically validate standard limits, allowing processors to focus entirely on exception resolution.
