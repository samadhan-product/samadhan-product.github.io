BFHL Service Guarantee — Lab & Doctor Booking Fulfilment Assurance

Case study: Designing an SFDC-led fulfilment assurance operating model to guarantee lab test and doctor appointment execution, reducing customer complaints.

Context

Following the OPD Reimbursement revamp, Samadhan led the Service Guarantee product initiative in 2022 at Bajaj Finserv Health Limited (BFHL). The scope covered doctor appointments and lab test bookings, orchestrating integrations across Salesforce (SFDC), HRx (core medical booking database), consumer apps, and operational queues.

Business problem

BFHL customers could easily book services digitally, but booking did not guarantee fulfilment. Provider confirmation, appointment execution, rescheduling, and cancellation tracking were handled manually over disconnected channels. There was no real-time status tracking, and provider no-shows or rejections left customers stranded.

First Principles Analysis: We reframed the product thesis: the product is not a booking system; it is a service assurance system. The customer's journey is incomplete when they book; it is complete only when they successfully receive the medical service. The product had to transition from booking creation to active fulfilment tracking, tracking provider responses and offering automated recovery paths.

User or operational pain

Operational teams had zero visibility into pending provider confirmations or SLA breaches. Customers suffered from high post-booking anxiety, leading to broken journeys, booking drop-offs, and a high volume of support complaints.

Product intervention

We built a complete fulfilment assurance layer:

  • SFDC Case Management Layer: Automatically converted every booking into an active, tracked case.
  • Provider Confirmation Tracker: Systematized provider accept, reject, reschedule, and no-response states.
  • Automated Communication: Triggered proactive WhatsApp, SMS, and Voice-Blast updates at key milestones.
  • TAT-Based Escalation: Programmed alerts for provider delays, moving cases automatically to agent queues.

AI capability used

The system utilized automated state synchronizations, TAT-based escalation rule triggers, and automated voice-blast integration rules to recover unconfirmed bookings.

Samadhan's role

As Lead PM, Samadhan owned the complete product solutioning. Supported by junior PMs, he defined the SFDC case status flows, authored the HRx-SFDC integration requirements, designed customer communication triggers, and led cross-functional execution until go-live.

Business outcome

The complete Service Guarantee flow went live successfully. Fulfilment Rate was established as the primary success metric. The initiative drastically reduced customer complaints, improved provider response TAT, and built trust in BFHL's booking platform.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Align the metric with the value: Shifting the north star metric from "bookings created" to "fulfilment rate" aligned the product, engineering, and operations teams on the true customer outcome.
  2. Design for recovery: Service failures (like doctor cancellations) are inevitable. Designing automated recovery paths (rescheduling slots, instant notification) is what preserves customer trust.
