BFHL Partner Center Revamp — Unified Provider Platform

Case study: Revamping the legacy Provider Portal into a unified partner self-serve platform across HealthPay, Labs, Hospitals, and Access Control.

Context

BFHL HealthPay 2023. Revamped the Provider Portal into Partner Center to serve labs, diagnostics networks, hospital partners, and merchants.

Business problem

Partners were heavily dependent on Relationship Managers (RMs) and WhatsApp follow-ups for basic visibility into transaction status, settlements, appointments, and hospital leads.

User or operational pain

Lack of self-service slowed HealthPay adoption. Multiple users sharing a single login ID reduced accountability, while support tickets were handled through informal, untracked channels.

Product intervention

Designed a cohort-based unified operating platform: self-serve HealthPay order/settlement views, lab appointment management with report upload, hospital lead workflows, and role-based access control.

AI capability used

Role-based access controls mapping center-wise user permissions, structured support SR routing, and dynamic data-export filters.

Samadhan's role

Samadhan led the initiative as Lead PM, managing two PMs: defined the roadmap, prioritized user stories, aligned cross-functional sales/operations teams, and drove partner adoption.

Business outcome

Partner Center went live across all tracks. Signficantly reduced RM/support dependence by enabling self-serve transaction, settlement, and appointment workflows, improving provider trust.

Lessons for other companies

Product value is about operational autonomy. Revamp workflows to replace human relationship manager bottlenecks with transparent, self-serve data portals.
