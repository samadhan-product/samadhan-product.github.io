BFHL Partner Center Revamp — Unified Provider Platform

Case study: Revamping the legacy Provider Portal into a unified partner self-serve platform across HealthPay, Labs, Hospitals, and Access Control.

Context

Under HealthPay in 2023, following the Recon Automation project, Samadhan led the complete revamp of the Provider Portal into Partner Center. This revamped platform was designed to serve BFHL's diagnostic networks, lab groups (such as Krsnaa, Yoda, ActivRx), hospitals, and HealthPay merchants.

Business problem

The legacy portal was a narrow access layer. Partners were highly dependent on Relationship Managers (RMs) and manual operations support to track booking status, download settlement reports, or resolve customer issues. Additionally, diagnostic networks frequently shared single user IDs across multiple physical centers, leaving no audit trail of who performed actions.

First Principles Analysis: The challenge was not just a UI design issue. Partners did not need a login; they needed operational autonomy. The product thesis had to shift: move partner operations from manual coordination to a self-serve partner operating center. The platform had to support multiple user hierarchies, Superuser controls, center-wise QR tracking, and dynamic payment links to enable field collections.

User or operational pain

RMs and support desks spent significant time handling basic partner information requests. Diagnostic networks experienced high order disputes due to shared logins. Phlebos and field agents lacked POS machines, relying on offline WhatsApp communication for payment confirmations.

Product intervention

We designed and launched Partner Center across six major cohorts:

  • HealthPay Orders & Settlements: Self-serve orders view, settlement details, QR filters, payment modes, and downloads.
  • Lab Appointment Management: Appointment status tracking, accept/decline actions, notifications, and report uploads.
  • Hospital Lead & Claims Management: Mapped hospital leads and claims assistance workflows.
  • Access Control: Mapped user hierarchies, center-level access, and superuser rules to address shared login issues.
  • Help & Support: Self-serve service request (SR) ticket creation.

AI capability used

Built role-based access control models mapping diagnostic center hierarchies and automated ticketing classification routing.

Samadhan's role

As Lead PM, Samadhan owned the complete Partner Center roadmap, user stories, GTM strategy, and stakeholder alignment. He led a team of two PMs (Maaz Modak and Abhishek Behera) and drove adoption directly with large diagnostic networks.

Business outcome

The revamp successfully consolidated partner touchpoints into a unified self-serve operating model. Partner Adoption was the primary metric. The platform reduced RM and support desk dependencies, improved partner trust, and supported HealthPay transaction volume growth.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Design for operational realities: Field research revealed that phlebos rely on QR codes mapped to specific centers for WhatsApp confirmation. Surfacing QR-level filters and downloadable reports in the portal resolved this issue.
  2. Build a foundation: Revamp the portal architecture to support multi-tenant cohorts (Labs, Hospitals, Merchants) from day one.
