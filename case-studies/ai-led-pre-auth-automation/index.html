AI-Led Pre-Authorisation Automation for Insurance Workflows

Case study: reducing pre-authorisation turnaround and operational load through workflow redesign, rules, and AI-assisted decision support in health insurance operations.

Context

Vidal Health TPA modernization under Bajaj Finserv Health. The project focused on the IPD Cashless Pre-Auth workflow covering fresh authorization, interim enhancement, and final discharge-related processing.

Business problem

Pre-authorization requests were document-heavy, clinical-rule dependent, and time-sensitive. Operational queues grew rapidly with network expansion. Teams relied on fragmented systems and manual, inconsistent document checks.

User or operational pain

Operations leaders faced rising Average Handling Time (AHT) and queue aging. Hospital partners escalated slow response times on discharge approvals, while internal medical teams burned capacity on repeatable administrative checks.

Product intervention

Product-led workflow modernization: queue strategy (Discharge Fast-Lane), document intelligence (chronological viewer and missing-document flags), and medical decision support with a rule engine positioning as the single source of truth for allowed amount sequencing.

AI capability used

Chronological document classification and chronological viewer synchronization, clinical signal summarization, ICD/PCS coding validation, and rule-based decision support with override triggers.

Samadhan's role

Samadhan led the product solutioning and workflow discovery: mapped granular Pre-Auth tasks, defined rule-engine sequencing, designed document viewer and queue strategies, and aligned clinical, operations, and engineering teams.

Business outcome

Successfully rolled out a structured modernization blueprint. Reduced Pre-Auth Fresh and Discharge TAT risk, improved processor productivity, and enforced rule compliance to prevent manual deduction bypass.

Lessons for other companies

Start with workflow truth, not model demos. Enforce rule sequencing at the system level to protect financial controls. Keep human experts in the loop for complex exceptions.
