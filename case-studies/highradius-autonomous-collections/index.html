HighRadius Autonomous Collections & Receivables Automation

Case study: Designing AI-driven worklists, automated outreach, AR inbox classification, and AP portal integrations for enterprise Order-to-Cash teams.

Context

This case study represents Samadhan's contribution as a Senior Product Manager at HighRadius from November 2021 to June 2022. Working in the Autonomous Receivables / Collections Management area, the scope covered designing intelligence-led Collections platforms for global enterprise finance teams (Office of the CFO).

Business problem

Enterprise collectors spent more than 40% of their day sorting through static aging reports, manually logging into customer accounts payable (AP) portals, sending repetitive reminders, and checking email boxes. Context was scattered across spreadsheets, ERPs, and outlook threads.

First Principles Analysis: Collections is a prioritization and workflow problem, not just a system of record. Collections teams do not need lists of unpaid bills; they need a system of prioritized action that automatically determines whom to contact, when, using what communication strategy, and what evidence is required to resolve collection disputes.

User or operational pain

Enterprise credit teams faced high past-due balances and poor cash predictability. Shared services leaders struggled with inconsistent, regional process variation across fragmented ERP instances (SAP, NetSuite, Oracle), while collectors suffered from high manual workloads.

Product intervention

We designed and rolled out the Autonomous Collections execution workbench:

  • AI-Driven Worklist Prioritization: Replaced static reports with risk/impact-based collector queues, ranking accounts by aging, payment likelihood, and dispute risk.
  • Automated Outreach Sequences: Scaled collection coverage for long-tail accounts using rule-based reminders and personalized templates.
  • AP Portal Automation: Built integrations to automatically submit invoices and track status within customer AP portals.

AI capability used

The platform utilized predictive worklist ranking algorithms, AR email inbox NLP classification (remittance vs dispute vs promise-to-pay), and automated invoice matching rules.

Samadhan's role

As Senior Product Manager, Samadhan led product discovery and definition for key modules of the workbench. He authored user stories, defined worklist priority rules, mapped AP portal status transitions, and collaborated with engineering on multi-ERP connector pipelines.

Business outcome

Helped transform collections from a reactive process into an intelligence-led execution model. Enabled global collections teams to increase portfolio call/email coverage, reduce manual tracking effort, and improve DSO predictability.

Lessons for other companies

Key Product Leadership Takeaways:

  1. Integrate the tool in the workspace: Collectors should not have to leave their dashboard to dial calls or reply to emails. Consolidation of inbox, dialer, and ledger context drives productivity.
  2. Structure dunning sequences: Automate the routine follow-ups so collectors can dedicate their specialized relationship skills to high-value collections disputes.
